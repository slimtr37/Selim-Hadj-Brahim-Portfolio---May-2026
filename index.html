Approach

How I think about content

Every piece of content I produce follows a framework built on intent, trust, and conversion — not word counts.

01

Start with search intent

Before writing a word, I map the exact question the reader is asking, the emotional state they are in, and the action they need to take next. Content that misreads intent fails regardless of how well it is written.

02

Build trust before asking

Whether the reader is a patient considering surgery abroad or a procurement manager sourcing industrial equipment, trust is the only currency that converts. I build it through specificity, credibility signals, and empathy.

03

Earn organic visibility

Good content earns links, rankings, and return visits. I structure every article to satisfy search engines by first satisfying the reader. Technical SEO without readability is noise. Readability without structure is invisible.

04

React to news fast

For lead generation clients in energy and telecom, timing is a competitive moat. When prices spike or regulation changes, I produce publishable content within 48 hours to capture the search wave before competitors react.

05

Adapt to market and culture

The Swiss market is not a translation of the French market. A GCC patient has different trust signals than a European patient. I build content that adapts to cultural context, not just language, across every market I serve.

06

Deliver measurable output

I track what matters: organic traffic, lead volume, inquiry rate, and content coverage across the buyer journey. Every content system I build is designed to produce a measurable outcome, not just content for its own sake.
